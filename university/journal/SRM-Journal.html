Journal Overview

This journal documents the key learnings, reflections, and experiences gained during the Security and Risk Management module.

Unit 1: Introduction to Security and Risk Management

Unit 1 offered an introduction to security and risk management by exploring foundational definitions and concepts. The unit reviewed key definitions of risk, discussed the risk management process, and introduced methods for assessing, qualifying, and mitigating risks. Various approaches to quantifying and qualifying risks were examined, alongside common security and risk standards. The insights gained from this unit, along with the accompanying lecture cast, are essential for understanding and applying qualitative and quantitative risk assessments in future contexts. This foundational knowledge will facilitate more effective risk management and security measures in practical scenarios.

Unit 2: Users, Assessments and the Risk Management Process

Unit 2 delves deeper into the Risk Management Process (RMP), focusing on the distinctions between qualitative and quantitative assessments and the critical role of user participation. The unit highlights how different assessment methods impact the RMP and underscore the necessity of engaging users throughout the process. The seminar provides a platform to reflect on the importance of these assessment approaches and user involvement, fostering a more comprehensive understanding of how these factors influence risk mitigation strategies. The knowledge gained is essential for effectively tailoring the RMP to specific contexts, ensuring more accurate and participatory risk management practices. . Download Threat Modelling Exercises

Unit 3: Introduction to Threat Modelling and Management

Unit 3 provides an in-depth exploration of threat modelling and management, distinguishing between threats and vulnerabilities while introducing key modelling techniques such as STRIDE, DREAD, Attack Trees, and hybrid models like PASTA. The unit emphasises the importance of selecting appropriate frameworks tailored to specific scenarios, supported by guides and cookbooks for practical application. Understanding these methodologies equips students with the ability to identify, assess, and manage threats effectively, ensuring robust security strategies. The insights gained from this unit is crucial for advising on the best threat modelling techniques and determining when to employ hybrid models for enhanced security management.

Unit 4: Application of Threat Modelling and Management Techniques

Unit 4 focuses on the practical application of threat modelling and management techniques, utilising resources such as the Threat Modelling Manifesto, the OWASP Cookbook, and the Mitre ATT&CK database. The unit provides an overview of various tools and resources available for threat modelling, evaluates their effectiveness, and offers guidance on selecting appropriate tools for specific scenarios. By creating threat models based on given scenarios, students gain critical insights into the application and evaluation of these tools. Mastery of these techniques is essential for professionals in cybersecurity and development, underpinning effective threat management and mitigation strategies.

Unit 5: An Introduction to Security and Risk Standards in Industry and the Enterprise

Unit 5 provides an in-depth exploration of industry and enterprise security and risk standards, emphasising their significance in risk assessments and designs. The module examines various industry-specific standards, the implications of these standards on security practices, and their integration into threat models. By understanding and applying these standards, professionals can enhance risk management strategies and ensure compliance with regulatory requirements. The unit also includes practical exercises, such as analysing GDPR case studies, to illustrate the application of security standards in real-world scenarios and develop strategies for effective risk mitigation. Unit 6: The Practical Implications of Security and Risk Standards

Unit 6 delves into the practical implications of security and risk standards, particularly focusing on GDPR compliance across various industries. Through the analysis of case studies and industry-specific standards, the module emphasises the critical role of regulatory frameworks in shaping risk management strategies. The unit highlights the importance of selecting the appropriate standards and developing effective mitigation plans to ensure compliance. A Development Team Project: Risk Identification Report was also submitted, reinforcing the practical application of theoretical concepts in real-world scenarios. This project further solidifies the understanding of integrating standards into risk management processes. Download Development Team Project Final

Unit 7: An Introduction to the Concepts of Quantitative Risk Modelling

RUnit 7 introduces quantitative risk modelling (QRM) by exploring probabilistic approaches such as Monte Carlo simulations and Bayes theorem-based methods, alongside decision analysis techniques like TOPSIS, AHP, and ANP. The unit focuses on selecting appropriate methods for risk modelling based on specific real-world scenarios and critically evaluating each approach's advantages and limitations. In addition, an initial post was submitted for the Spring et al. (2021) reading, addressing the critique of CVSS characteristics and discussing an alternative model. This unit's content equips individuals with essential tools to apply QRM techniques effectively in various risk management contexts. Download Intial Post

Unit 8: Implementing Quantitative Risk Models.

Unit 8 explores the practical implementation of quantitative risk modelling (QRM) techniques, specifically focusing on Monte Carlo simulations and Bayes Theorem. The unit emphasises the application of QRM in project management and safety estimation, with critical evaluations of various techniques. By engaging with real-world case studies, this unit equips individuals with the ability to select and apply the most appropriate modelling techniques for specific scenarios. A quantitative risk model was developed and critically appraised. Peer responses were submitted to further discuss the application of QRM in various contexts, fostering collaborative learning and critical analysis of the approaches studied. Download Peer Responses

Unit 9: Risk, Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery

Unit 9 covers the critical aspects of business continuity (BC) and disaster recovery (DR) planning, focusing on key elements such as Business Impact Assessments (BIA), Recovery Time Objectives (RTO), and Recovery Point Objectives (RPO). The practical importance of creating robust BC and DR plans is emphasised, along with the exploration of emerging trends in information risk management. The ability to identify and suggest suitable solutions to meet recovery objectives is a key outcome of this unit. Additionally, a summary post was submitted as part of a collaborative learning discussion, further enhancing understanding through peer engagement and reflection. Download Summery Post

Unit 10: Introduction to Threat Modelling and Management

Unit 10 delves into the practical implementation of disaster recovery (DR) solutions, focusing on the critical role of Recovery Point Objectives (RPO) and Recovery Time Objectives (RTO) in shaping DR strategies. The unit explores typical system designs to meet standby requirements, along with their limitations. Additionally, challenges such as vendor lock-in, resilience, and network security are addressed. A critical review of Disaster Recovery as a Service (DRaaS) highlights its advantages and disadvantages. Moreover, the completion of activities Part A and B provided hands-on experience in designing DR solutions that meet specific RPO and RTO criteria, reinforcing theoretical knowledge with practical application.
